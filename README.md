## The Aegis Project

We are a small collective of independent engineers and enthusiasts who believe the modern internet has taken an unfortunate turn toward mandatory identity verification and centralized oversight. Aegis exists as a response to the ever-growing reach of corporate and state censorship, where the simple act of private communication has been transformed into a series of guarded interactions. Our goal is to provide a sovereign ecosystem where the right to speak and associate is protected by the objective laws of mathematics rather than the shifting whims of a moderation board. We have designed our system around a zero-trust model where the server is treated as a hostile but necessary message router: it handles the logistics of delivery but possesses no cryptographic capability to peer into the content of your conversations.
The foundation of our work is the Aegis Secure Communication Protocol (ASCP), a federated-ready system that prioritizes engineering integrity over modern industry hype. We do not care about your age, your location, or your government-issued identification: we provide the tools for private association and leave the responsibility of their use entirely to the participants. While we maintain a healthy skepticism of most contemporary technological trends, we have found that our collective hatred for the current state of the javascript ecosystem far outweighs our distaste for artificial intelligence. Consequently, we have reached a compromise: we allow machines to wrestle with the visual tedium of css and frontend components so that our human engineers can focus their remaining sanity on the backend logic and cryptographic security of the protocol.
The core of the project is built using rust and go for the backend and typescript with webassembly for the frontend client. We prefer robust, somewhat pedantic implementations of established cryptographic primitives over experimental "web3" gimmicks. If you value privacy, appreciate a good argument about memory safety, and don't mind a bit of cynical humor, you are welcome to explore our repositories and contribute to a web that remembers its original purpose.

## AI Usage Policy

To maintain the security and auditability of the Aegis ecosystem, we have established a strict boundary for the use of large language models within our codebase. The policy is simple: ai may be used exclusively for the development of the frontend client, specifically for the generation of styling, layout components, and purely visual adjustments. We recognize that modern web development is a chaotic landscape of boilerplate, and we are willing to delegate that specific burden to the machines.

However, the use of ai-generated code is strictly prohibited in any area involving the core backend, the cryptographic modules, or the protocol definitions themselves. Every line of code concerning key exchange, encryption, and the double ratchet implementation must be authored and signed off by a human being. We do not trust probabilistic models to handle deterministic security: we prefer our bugs to be the result of human error we can actually learn from rather than a statistical hallucination. Any pull request found to be using machine-generated logic for backend routing or security-sensitive functions will be rejected: we do not gamble with the integrity of the protocol.
